Northridge is located in the Northwest part of the San Fernando Valley and is sheltered by the Santa Susana Mountains . Most of the real estate in Northridge is level land. However, on the north end of the community known as Porter Ranch, there are homes on mountainous terrain. Northridge is located about 30 miles northwest of downtown Los Angeles . The community is comprised of 17 square miles.

The average Northridge home for sale is priced is at $689,000. However, Northridge properties can start as low as $299,000 for a condominium and go as high as $3,100,000 for a luxurious private estate.

Early Northridge California revolved around a watering hole that was located in the area between what is today Roscoe and Parthenia Boulevards. It is where the Gabrielino Indians and later the Spanish, who explored Missions in the area, congregated. Northridge was the only San Fernando Valley station on the Southern Pacific Railroad line in the late 1800's.

Once water was brought to the Valley, Northridge was primarily an agricultural area. The groves of orange trees that stand in front of California State University Northridge were originally planted in the 1920's. They are some of the last remaining groves from the San Fernando Valley 's early days. In the 1940's and 50's, the community became known as "The Horse Capital of the World,” because of the horse races and shows at Devonshire Downs.

Four of the San Fernando Valley 's major institutions are located in the Northwest. California State University , Northridge, established in the late 1950's, has grown from a small branch college into a nearly 28,000 student university with some unique world recognized programs. The Northridge Medical Center has added to the area's value because of an outstanding medical facility and programs, including its Trauma Center . Retail sales were boosted with the building of the largest shopping mall in the valley opened in 1971, the Northridge Fashion Center . The fourth is the Los Angeles Police Devonshire Division, which has gained the reputation of providing one of the safest and lowest crime areas of all of Los Angeles .

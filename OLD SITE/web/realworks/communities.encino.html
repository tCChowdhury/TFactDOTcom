Santa Catalina de Bononia de Los Encinos, known as "Encino".  Encino is proud of its history and of the beautiful community it has become.  Encino remains the jewel of the San Fernando Valley, a community of lovely Spanish style homes on rolling hills, at the foot of the Santa Monica Mountains .   Although it is growing with its vital business district, it maintains its caring calm atmosphere.

The average city of Encino real estate listing is priced in the mid $900,000. However, Encino properties can start as low as $185,000 for an Encino condo and go as high as $11,900,000 for a luxurious private estate.

Local residents enjoy the warm summers and mild winters.  Not to mention the wide range of local activities, and historical parks and museums made up of 5 acres of tranquil land set aside for the area's heritage.  The residents enjoy many different types of arts and crafts along with sporting events and safe clean playgrounds for the children.  Residents can enjoy an 18-hole golf course, bicycle paths, garden center and 20 acres of picnic grounds, 60 acres of wildlife park with an 11 acre pond.

Encino has accommodations to more than 50 different restaurants along Ventura Boulevard making it the " Valley of Restaurants Row ".  Not to mention that most of the business district is located within a 3.2 mile stretch of the city.  Within minutes you have access to the 101 Freeway which runs east and west of the valley, or the 405 will take you into downtown Los Angeles , to the Santa Monica beaches, and to the Los Angeles International Airport .

The family oriented community has drawn over 40,000 middle class and upper income residents.  The community has an excellent school system, and top rate health care systems.  Encino has maintained its independence as a business center with the greatest number of banks and savings & loans of any San Fernando Valley community.
